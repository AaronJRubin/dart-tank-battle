About this Game

This is a simple tank shooting game, implemented in the Dart programming language and designed to be played by multiple players around a single keyboard. The source code is available at my github.

The controls were designed to minimize the amount of keys that would need to be processed simultaneously. No more than two keys should be pressed at the same time per player! This concern motivated my decision to make shooting automatic, rather than player-controlled.

Most keyboards only allow for up to six key-presses to be registered at once. Consequently, playing this game with four players, which would require up to eight simultaneous key-presses, is not recommended unless you have a very high-end keyboard.

It should also be noted that transparency (needed for particle effects like those in the Nine Pillar Lightning Stage) does not always work as it should, depending upon a variety of WebGL performance-related factors that are outside of the programmer's control. Details about this issue are discussed in the README at the github repository. Here, let it suffice to say that if you encounter some troubles with invisible obstacles in the Nine Pillar Lightning Stage, this is the reason! Depending upon your computer's GPU, however, you may not have any troubles at all.

While the actual models used in this game were created by me from geometric primitives (spheres, cylinders, etc.), the textures were downloaded from the internet. My sources were the following:

  • The texture used for the energy balls in the Nine Pillar Lightning Stage was accessed from here.
  • The texture used for the cloud floor of the Nine Pillar Stage was obtained from here. The photographer's flickr can be accessed here.
  • The incredible skybox used for the Nine Pillar Stage, entitled "Night Sky", was accessed from this page. Unfortunately, I could not determine the original artist's name.
  • The texture used for the floor of the Lava Death Stage was made by Twister10, and accessed from here.
  • The texture used for the lava balls and the lava pool in the Lava Death Stage was accessed from here. I do not believe that Mr./Ms. Stemkoski made this texture himself, however, as this texture seems to be common in WebGL examples. I haven't been able to find authoritative information as to the original creator!
  • The texture used for the volcano material in the Lava Death Stage was made by Duion, released on opengameart.org. Here is a direct link to the texture's page. The texture is apparently based on a photo by someone whom Duion only names as "Williams."
  • The texture used to signify that a player character has been "burned" in the Lava Death Stage was made by someone named mad-texture, and released on his deviantart. Here's a link to the page!

  • Making shooting a player-initiated event would require increasing the amount of simultaneous key-presses from multiple players at once, so that is also off the table for the time being.
